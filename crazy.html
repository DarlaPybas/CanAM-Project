Who is Aaron Hedges

Project Blog About Home

September 3rd 2014

38 year old Elk Hunter Aaron Joseph Hedges went on a hunting trip with two friends, Greg Leitner and Joe Depew. Taking along two horses, a mule their guns, bows and packs they started their way down Cottonwood Lake Trailhead, toward Campfire Lake. Along the way, the mule gets spooked by something and throws all of Aaron's things off of it's back. They were able to gather everything except his sleeping bag. He had roughed it before however so this was no big deal to Aaron.

September 5th, 2014

After one night of no sleeping bag Aaron decided to go up to one of their cache spots nearby where they kept extra camping gear- including a sleeping bag. So he headed off to Sunset Lake with his walkie-talkie, cellphone, firearm and bow. Nobody worried about him as he was an experienced outdoorsman, having grown up there. This, however, would be the last time he was ever seen alive again.

The two friends waited all day and even the whole next day before even beginning to worry about him. Luckily, when they did start to worry they just grabbed out their walkie talkie that had a GPS and saw where he was. He had missed his turn at a fork in the road, they just guessed he would notice that he had taken a wrong turn and come back since Aaron knew the place so well.

September 8th, 2014

They finally report Aaron missing as nothing has turned up. Immediately a search was mounted and even though the snow had slowed them down they still scoured the area with helicopters with infrared systems, dog teams and horses but no sign of Aaron.

September 10th, 2014

With not a lot of hope left with the snowy conditions, they finally find a small sign. But, it did not make matters better. By a creek east of Sunlight Lake, there were remnants of where someone had tried to start a fire using a pack of cigarettes, Aaron's water bladder, waist strips off his backpack and his boots neatly and intentionally placed side by side. All of these findings were very unusual for searchers especially the straps from his backpack. What was even more puzzling was the fact that searchers had just went down that path the day before and the belongings were not there.

June 15, 2015

After months of searching and no other signs of Aaron, the search was called off. Until a hiker named Roger Beslanowitch was walking near a relatives ranch and came across a backpack with all of Aaron's belongins including: his cellphone, an orange hunting vest, bow, clothes, hunting license and granola bar wrappers. Just ahead of him, he also spotted a thermos cup and an open energy drink nearby. After this report the cold case was reopened and the search began again, but to no avail.

August 6th, 2016

Finally after all the time of searching, Aaron Hedges' remains would be found. They would be found a half a mile from where his gear had been found by Roger. Not only was he 15 miles away from where he orginially went missing, but he was also six miles away from where his boots were. Meaning, he trecked in dense thickets with blankets of snow on the ground barefooted. What has also perplexed many is that where his gear was found, including his thermos, you could see the ranch nearby and a frequently used road. Which means he saw his safety and never went to it. Why?
