Who is Thomas Messick

Project Blog About Home

November 15, 2015

Thomas Messick and six friends entered the woods near Horicon on November 15th. All were equppied with their guns, bows, walkie talkies and packs full of food and water. Not only was Messick an Army veteran but he also was an experienced outdoorsman, so much so that he once served as a hunter safety instructor. They all decided to split off and part of them men went to the top of the hill (to push wildlife down the hill) and the others stayed at the bottom ready to shoot. All hunters were maybe 100 ft apart from one another. This was the first time they have done any hunting in this area, but most deifinitely not their first time hunting. It was described as a good day to hunt.

2:00pm

By this time everyone was supposed to meet back up, and everyone did except Thomas Messick. They waited an hour for him and at 3pm, they began to search. They drove through the woods beeping theirs horns and yelling for Thomas, exactly where he had been only hours ago.

4:30pm-Next Day

The group of hunters has now realized they need help. They call and alert forest rangers who then searched all night for Messick. The other hunters tried to reach out via the walkie-talkie, but nothing came back. They shot their guns and flairs, but to no avail. There were over 180 people looking for him. They searched 1.5 miles in every direction of Messicks last known location.

7 Years Later

The search went on for months, finally with no sign they called it off. There has been no sighting of any of his belongings or him.
