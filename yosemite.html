Who is Sandra Johnsen-Hughes

Project Blog About Home

June 2020

Sandra Hughes was an avid outdoors woman. She trained to be a park ranger and any chance she got she was outside. After college life became mundane for her, she became an accountant instead of following her dreams.

At the time of her disappearance she had been living, working in Maui, Hawaii. She decided to pick up her dream again and decided to treck to Yosemite and stay there for a couple weeks. Her family wished her luck and worried not about her as they knew the experience she had. June 26th, 2020- that was the last day they saw or heard from her.

July 2nd, 2020

Hughes camping gear, sleeping bag and other supplies are found strewn all over by hikers near Johnson Meadow- where she said she would be. But no sign of Sandra. Her niece advised something had to be wrong as Sandra was a very tidy person and would never just leave her things in an array as they were.

July 3rd, 2020

A couple of hikers spot a barefooted woman with a bruise on her face. They offered assistance not knowing if she was okay, but she declined. They later found out it was Sandra Hughes. When searchers looked at the place of sighting she was again gone.

July 5th, 2020

Hughes car, a silver Saab, was found appearing to have crashed into a tree then rolled into the rave at Chiquito Creek, which was just north of her campsite. Searchers combed the area. Nothing

July 12th, 2020

Her sleeping bag is found just outside of Yosemite National Park, North of where her car was spotted. At this time, the searchers were reduced to a small number as they dod not expect to find anything. They were right.

August 9th, 2020

Two hunters are driving along road 5S01 near Portuguege Creek. One of them spots a woman leaning against a tree wearing a floral shirt and overalls, drinking water. She was not distressed and avoided contact with the men. This is the last known sighting of Sandra Hughes.
